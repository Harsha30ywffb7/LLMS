Generative models: Creating a cohrent answer

- unlike retrival models generate sentences that make sense together.
purpose: generative models build sentences word by word for natural answers.

How does they work:
Generative models predict each words sequentially and creating a smooth and logical sentence.

how they solve: 
coherence
- connects all information into sentences for easy understanding.
adaptability:
- can adjusts responses based on topic or complexity , like simple explanations when asked.

Breaking down generative models:
- Using attention
- predicting next word - predicts one word at a time.
- decoding the response - continues word predictions untill , meaningful answers created.






RAG architecture -- Retrieval and Gen Models in RAG.

benifits of combining retrieval and gen models:
more clarity on answers.


How retrieval augments the generative model:

without retrieval: model alone can generate the answers based on the prior training, potentially lead to inaccuracies.
with retrieval: the generative model has specific , relevant information to use , increasing accuracy in response.

augmentation effect: the retreival step grounds the generative model, making the final answer both clear and correct.


End to end workflow: RAG pipelines in action.
step1: user input question --> step-2: transform question into vector( creates Embeddings) -> step-3: retrive relevant info( similarity matching) -->step-4:(feed info to gen model) these all retrieved info used by the next step by coherence -->applying attention(within in the retrived info)--> step-5: generates the answer--> decoding the response. --> output to the user.
